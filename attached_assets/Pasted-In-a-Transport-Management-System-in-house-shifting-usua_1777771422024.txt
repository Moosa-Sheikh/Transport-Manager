In a Transport Management System, “in-house shifting” usually means internal/local movement of goods/assets within the same company or client network, not a normal commercial trip.

Example:

A company says:

Move stock from Warehouse A to Warehouse B
Shift goods from factory to showroom
Move material from one yard/godown to another
Transfer goods internally without customer freight billing

So it is still a transport movement, but it is not always treated like a revenue trip.

How professional TMS systems usually handle it

They normally add it as a separate job type inside the trip system.

Instead of only:

Trip

They use something like:

Trip / Movement Type

With values such as:

Customer Freight Trip
In-House Shifting
Empty Vehicle Movement
Maintenance Movement
Recommended structure for your app

You do not need a totally separate system. Add it inside the existing Trips Module.

1. Add movement type to trips

In the trips table:

movement_type

Example values:

customer_trip
in_house_shifting

For normal customer trips, the trip has customer, freight income, bilty, payments, profit, etc.

For in-house shifting, the trip may have:

From location
To location
Department / warehouse
Truck
Driver
Date
Material description
Quantity / weight
Fuel expense
Labour expense
Other expense
Remarks
Difference from normal trip
Normal Customer Trip
Customer pays freight
Income is calculated
Customer payment is tracked
Profit = Freight income - expenses
In-House Shifting
No external customer payment
Usually no freight receivable
Only internal cost is tracked
Cost may be charged to department / warehouse / project
Profit may not apply, or profit is negative cost

So for reporting, it becomes:

Internal shifting cost report
Truck-wise internal cost
Department-wise shifting cost
Warehouse-to-warehouse movement history
Driver-wise shifting work
Tables you may add

Keep your existing trips table, but add a few supporting tables.

Suggested new tables
locations

For warehouses, godowns, factories, yards, branches.

departments

For internal cost centers.

trip_internal_details

Only used when movement_type = 'in_house_shifting'.

Example fields:

trip_id
from_location_id
to_location_id
department_id
material_description
quantity
weight
purpose
approved_by
Better professional design

Instead of putting too many nullable fields in trips, keep trips as the common base table.

trips
├── normal customer trip details
├── trip_loads
├── trip_expenses
└── trip_internal_details

So every movement is still a trip, but the system knows its type.

UI structure

In the app, add this in the sidebar:

Trips
  - Create Customer Trip
  - Create In-House Shifting
  - Trip List

Or use one create form with:

Movement Type:
[Customer Trip] [In-House Shifting]

When the user selects In-House Shifting, hide customer freight fields and show internal fields.
